About

I'm Pierre Allain, an engineer working at the intersection of AI and software systems. I've spent most of my career building technical products and thinking about how intelligent systems can be made useful, reliable, and understandable.

My background spans software engineering, machine learning, and product development. I'm drawn to problems where technical depth meets real-world complexity — the kind of work where getting the details right actually matters. I care about building things that are well-crafted, not just functional.

Outside of technology, I'm a distance runner and a consistent reader. I find that training for long races and reading widely both serve the same purpose: they create space for slow, sustained thinking. I also write occasionally — short notes on ideas, tools, books, and things I'm learning.

This site is a simple place to collect those threads. It's intentionally quiet. No newsletter, no metrics, no engagement loops. Just a homepage for someone who builds things and tries to think clearly about them.
